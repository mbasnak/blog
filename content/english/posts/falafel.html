This recipe yields about 15 falafel. The most important part is that you use dry chickpeas!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound dry chickpeas

  • 1/2 onion

  • 2 garlic cloves

  • 1 tbsp flour

  • 1 tsp comino

  • 1 tsp salt

  • pepper to taste

  • 1/3 cup chopped parsley

  • 1/3 cup chopped cilantro

Directions

  • Soak the chickpeas overnight with plenty of water.

  • The next day, drain and rinse them.

  • Put them in a blender or food processor with the onion, garlic, cumin, flour, cumin, salt, pepper, chopped cilantro and parsley.

  • Process everything well, but it shouldn’t get liquid and should still have texture.

  • Put the mix in the fridge for about 2 h or in the freezer for about 30 min.

  • Shape them and cook them in the oven at medium-high heat with some oil for about 25 min, flipping them half way through.

  • If you’re baking them, making them rather flat as in the picture. If you want to fry them, you can shape them into balls.

  • I served them with a dip of cream cheese, mil, curry and garlic powder, and another one with mayo, mustard, lemon and olive oil.
